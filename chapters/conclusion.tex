\section{Conclusion}


\lipsum[2-3]

This thesis designed and evaluated a cost-sensitive, CNN-based Intrusion Detection System (IDS) for the NSL-KDD dataset, with a particular focus on improving detection of minority attack classes (R2L, U2R) while maintaining strong overall performance. Starting from well-known issues of NSL-KDD—severe class imbalance, ageing traffic profiles, and the difficulty of KDDTest-21 ~\cite{naseer2018enhanced}, ~\cite{li2017intrusion}, ~\cite{zamani2013machine}, ~\cite{alkasassbeh2018machine}, ~\cite{li2018machine}—the work developed a transparent, reproducible pipeline covering curated data splits, consistent preprocessing, principled model design, and evaluation based on macro-averaged and per-class metrics.
